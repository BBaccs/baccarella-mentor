  • Learning Philosophy
    Their are 5 core principles to my leanring philosphy. I've learned these things the hard and slow way, now I'm sharing them with you.
    • 1
    • 2
    • 3
    • 4
    80/20 Rule
    We will be focusing on the 20% of programming that, if mastered, will produce 80% of the results. A.K.A the core princples, the fundamentals. This will also allow you to transfer into other languages and frameworks once you get your first Junior Dev position.
    Need to Know Basis
    After the fundamentals you need to progress on a need to know basis, depending on the current job market in your desired location, or skills needed to complete a project you're working on.
    Don't Reinvent the Wheel
    I share with you the courses, videos, and books that helped me learn programming. These are done by the industries best, the problem with them is you can't pause a video and ask questions. By going through them together, we can clear up misunderstanding and unanswered questions.
    Feynman Technique
    This technique to solidifying knowledge is to teach what you've learned, or are struggling to learn, to someone else. If you can break down a concept in simple terms and teach you're mentor, you've really learned the topic. Combine all of these principles and add a mentor and you're setting yourself for success.
